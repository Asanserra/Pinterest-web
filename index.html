For a standard 3-seat sofa, 20×20 inch pillows work best as your main set. A loveseat looks great with 18×18 inch pillows. Always mix textures — velvet with linen, or solid with a subtle pattern — for a layered, designer look. Don't overthink it: odd numbers (3 or 5 pillows) tend to look more natural than even arrangements.
A minimalist living room works best with a neutral base: warm whites, soft creams, or greige (grey-beige). Add warmth with natural wood tones and a single earthy accent — terracotta, sage green, or dusty blush. Stick to the 60/30/10 rule: 60% neutral walls and large furniture, 30% wood and natural textures, 10% accent color in cushions and accessories.

The designer coffee table formula: start with a tray to anchor everything, add a stack of 2–3 books, one organic element (a plant, dried pampas grass, or candle), and one sculptural object (a decorative bowl or small vase). Leave some negative space — don't over-fill. Aim for varying heights and mix materials like ceramic, wood, and metal.
The biggest 2025 home decor trends are: (1) Quiet Luxury — understated, high-quality neutral palettes; (2) Organic Modern — natural textures like linen, jute, and rattan with clean lines; (3) Warm Minimalism — minimalism with more warmth, wood, and tactile layers; (4) Biophilic Design — bringing nature indoors with plants, natural light, and earthy tones.
